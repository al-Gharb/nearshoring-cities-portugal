# EU Education & Training Monitor 2025 — Portugal Summary

**Source**: [European Commission Education and Training Monitor 2025](https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/portugal.html)  
**Relevance**: Official EU data on Portugal's STEM/ICT talent pipeline for nearshoring decisions

---

## Executive Snapshot

Portugal is strengthening its education system to respond to labour market needs. STEM enrolment slightly exceeds the EU average, and VET modernisation is underway. However, **labour shortages persist in engineering and ICT**, and female representation in these fields remains limited.

---

## Key STEM & ICT Statistics

### Higher Education STEM Enrolment (2023)

| Metric | Portugal | EU Average | EU 2030 Target |
|--------|----------|------------|----------------|
| STEM enrolment (% of HE students) | **28.9%** | 26.9% | 32% |
| Engineering share of STEM | **68.5%** | 54.6% | — |
| ICT PhD students (% of all PhDs) | 2.2% | 3.8% | 5% |

**Note**: Portugal's STEM share declined slightly from 30.0% (2015) to 28.9% (2023).

### Gender Gap in STEM

| Field | Women (PT) | Women (EU) | EU 2030 Target |
|-------|------------|------------|----------------|
| All STEM fields | 31.3% | 32.2% | 40% |
| Natural Sciences | **53.5%** | 51.5% | — |
| ICT | 18.4% | 20.3% | — |
| ICT (PhD level) | 22.3% | 24.3% | 33% |

**Insight**: Female participation in ICT has remained virtually unchanged since 2015 (18.3% → 18.4%).

## Graduate Output & Programmes

### Impulso Jovens STEAM Programme

- **Target**: Increase STEM graduates by 10,000 in first-cycle programmes and short-term courses
- **2023 Result**: **10,071 new STEM graduates** — exceeding forecast by 166%
- **Adult Training**: 20,000+ adults to be trained in cooperation with employers and HEIs

### VET (Vocational Education & Training)

| Metric | Portugal | EU Average | EU 2030 Target |
|--------|----------|------------|----------------|
| STEM in medium-level VET | 30.5% | 36.3% | 45% |
| Female in VET STEM | 10.9% | 15.4% | 25% |
| Work-based learning rate | **77.8%** | 65.2% | — |
| VET graduate employment rate | 75.5% | 80.0% | — |

**Investment**: €710 million RRP investment in VET modernisation, including 365 specialised technological centres.

## Labour Market Challenges

### Key Shortages Identified

1. **Engineering** — President of Portuguese Order of Engineers noted shortages in public sector
2. **Civil Engineering** — identified as one of the most understaffed professions
3. **ICT** — persistent shortages despite relatively strong enrolment

### Contributing Factors

- Financial constraints in public sector
- Low professional prestige for certain engineering fields
- Gender imbalance limiting talent pool

## Implications for Nearshoring

### Positives

✅ STEM enrolment exceeds EU average (28.9% vs 26.9%)  
✅ Engineering dominates STEM (68.5% of STEM students)  
✅ Strong work-based learning in VET (77.8% vs EU 65.2%)  
✅ Digital skills above EU average (ICILS 2023)  
✅ Massive RRP investment in tech education (€710M+ for VET alone)  
✅ Tertiary attainment nearly doubled in a decade  

### Challenges

⚠️ Labour shortages in engineering and ICT persist despite good enrolment  
⚠️ ICT PhD pipeline is weak (2.2% vs EU 3.8%)  
⚠️ Gender imbalance limits effective talent pool  
⚠️ VET graduate employment rate below EU average  
⚠️ Regional concentration (Lisbon Metro = 53% attainment; Azores = 22%)  

### Strategic Takeaway

Portugal's education pipeline is **improving but constrained**. The data suggests:

1. **Graduate supply is adequate** — 28.9% STEM enrolment, 10,000+ new STEM grads/year
2. **Demand outstrips supply** — ongoing shortages indicate strong absorption by domestic employers
3. **Engineering > pure ICT** — the talent pool skews toward broad engineering (68.5% of STEM)
4. **Regional strategy matters** — Lisbon Metro has 2.4× the tertiary attainment of peripheral regions

For nearshoring operations, this validates:
- Using **broad STEM/ICT definitions** (engineering + computer science + automation)
- Targeting **multiple cities** rather than just Lisbon to access underutilised regional talent
- Expecting **competition** for experienced hires (hence the focus on graduate flow data)
